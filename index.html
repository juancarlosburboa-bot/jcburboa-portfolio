  • The artist becomes the trader becomes the artist.★
    Trevor McFedries · X
    The only essay I've ever read that accurately diagnoses what's broken: artists grinding themselves into dust playing algorithm games when their real skill is reading culture and taking positions. The hot take is that trading infrastructure is more aligned with how artists think than our current content-marketing hellscape. Artists have ALWAYS gotten paid when the market validates their thesis, the difference now is they can finally capture that value directly instead of feeding it to galleries, labels, or platforms. Art is a tradable asset. Always has been.

How Big Things Get Done
Bent Flyvbjerg & Dan Gardner
Cracks the code on why megaprojects consistently fail while offering a deceptively simple fix: think slow, act fast. Sounds like a fortune cookie until you hit the Olympics case study: Bazillions over budget every single time because each host city insists on reinventing the wheel with an entirely new team. The core insight — that the planning phase is where you win or lose, not execution — feels obvious in hindsight but revolutionary in practice. Everyone believes they're immune to delays, overruns, and optimism bias. They're not. You're not. No one is. A must-read if you're trying to build something big.
